This course is designed to provide an overview of financial reporting. Financial reporting, defined broadly, is the transmission of information from companies to external users. "Companies" include small businesses, partnerships, corporations, and not-for-profits; "users" include investors, creditors, analysts, auditors, the government, and more. The overarching objective of A215 is to provide students with familiarity of financial accounting, including the mechanics of accounting, how accounting information can be used in decision making, and the role of accounting in business and more generally, in society as a whole.
